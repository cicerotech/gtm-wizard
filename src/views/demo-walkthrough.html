The Problem

Every sales org has this problem. Reps spend hours on admin that doesn't close deals. Notes live in random docs. CRM is stale. When leadership asks "what's happening with this account," the answer takes 10 minutes of digging.

Pacing

Set the stakes quickly. This is shared context -- the audience already feels this pain. Don't dwell. 90 seconds max, then move to the product.

Admin Overhead

Reps lose hours weekly on CRM updates, note organization, and meeting prep. That's time not spent with clients.

Scattered Context

Intelligence lives in Slack threads, email, personal notes, and Salesforce -- none of it connected. Context is lost between meetings.

Stale Visibility

Leadership reviews reveal outdated information. Forecasts rely on what reps remembered to log, not what actually happened.

Danger Zone

Do NOT get into "how" yet. If someone asks, use: "I'll show you exactly how in 60 seconds."

We built something that fixes this. Not a new tool reps have to learn -- an intelligence layer that works behind the scenes, connecting what already exists.

Transition

"Let me show you what this looks like in practice."

Why This Matters

Most sales orgs treat CRM as a reporting tool -- reps update it because they have to, not because it helps them. We flipped that. CRM updates itself. Intelligence flows to the rep, not the other way around.

Pacing

Land the three takeaways, then close with the live offer. This should feel like a natural conclusion, not a sales pitch. 90 seconds.

The Result

Three things this changes for the team:

More Time on High-Value Work

Reps spend time in meetings, at events, on creative account strategy -- not on data entry or meeting prep admin. The system handles the overhead.

Always-Current Intelligence

Every account brief reflects the latest Salesforce data, calendar events, and team notes. No stale context. No "let me pull that up."

Operational Edge

This is how a modern sales org operates. Most teams don't have this. It compounds -- the more reps use it, the better the intelligence gets.
