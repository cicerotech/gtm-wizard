Getting Started
Setup in 3 Steps
Download the vault, enter your email, connect Salesforce. Your accounts load automatically from SF -- no manual entry.

One-Time Setup

Enter your @eudia.com email and click Connect. The vault validates your calendar access, connects to Salesforce, and imports every account you own. Setup takes about 30 seconds.

Your Workspace
Account Folders
Every account you own in Salesforce gets a folder. Inside: Contacts, Intelligence, Meeting Notes, Next Steps, and Notes 1-3 -- all pre-populated from Salesforce.

Your sidebar. Accounts listed alphabetically. Click any to expand and see sub-notes. Pipeline and Recordings folders at the bottom.

Contacts. Auto-enriched from Salesforce -- names, titles, emails, phone numbers. Updated on each sync.

Next Steps. Completed tasks and open actions pulled from Salesforce automatically. Shows last 90 days of activity.

Meeting Notes. Pre-templated with account_id for SF sync. Date, Attendees, Discussion, and Next Steps sections ready to fill.

What's inside each account folder: Contacts (from SF), Intelligence (deal context + positioning), Meeting Notes (synced history), Next Steps (auto-pulled tasks), and Notes 1-3 (your working notes). New notes auto-name as "Note 4 - Feb 17" when you create them.

On Calls
Taking Meeting Notes
Open any Note file during a call. The template has your account linked and sections ready. New notes auto-name with the date.

Before the Call

Open the account folder. Review the Contacts note for stakeholder names and titles. Check Intelligence for deal context and positioning.

During the Call

Type notes in the Discussion section. Or click the mic icon in the left sidebar to record the call -- AI handles the rest.

After the Call

If you recorded, the AI summary generates in ~2 minutes. Add action items to Next Steps. Toggle sync_to_salesforce to push to the Account record.

What Syncs Back

Your notes land on the Account's Customer Brain field in Salesforce. Managers see them. GTM Brain includes them in future queries. Pipeline Review shows updated Next Steps.

AI Transcription
Recording a Meeting
Click the microphone icon in the left sidebar during a call. When you stop, AI transcribes and summarizes automatically.

How It Works

Click mic → recording starts (red indicator). Take notes normally. When the call ends, click Stop. The AI processes the audio and generates a structured summary.

What You Get

Structured summary with: Discussion Points, Key Takeaways, Next Steps, and Action Items. All properly formatted and ready to share or sync.

How Long It Takes

A 30-min call processes in about 2 minutes. A 60-min call takes about 4 minutes. You can navigate away -- the summary appears in the note when ready.

Safety

45-minute prompt asks if you're still recording. 90-minute auto-stop. Audio is processed server-side and not stored after transcription.

Connected
Salesforce Sync
Every note has a sync_to_salesforce toggle in its frontmatter. When enabled, the note content pushes to the Account's Customer Brain field in Salesforce.

What happens when you sync: Your meeting summary is prepended to the Account's Customer_Brain__c field. It's timestamped and attributed to you. Managers with vaults see it on their next refresh. GTM Brain includes it when anyone asks about this account. Pipeline Review picks up updated Next Steps.

Settings

Your email, timezone, Salesforce connection status, and server URL. One-time setup -- everything runs automatically after the initial connect. Click Reconnect if your SF session expires.
