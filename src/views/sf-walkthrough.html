How It All Connects

From prospect to closed deal to delivery. Here's the full lifecycle of a deal in our Salesforce.

Account → Opportunity → Work the Deal → Delivery (S4) → Contract (Won) → Revenue
Step 1
The Account
Every company is an Account. Contacts, open opportunities, CSM notes, and account health -- all on one page.

Contacts

Key stakeholders with titles, emails, and roles. These feed into GTM Brain and meeting prep.

Opportunities

All active and closed deals. ACV, stage, and target dates visible at a glance.

CSM Section

Account health, history, and customer overview. Managed by Customer Success.

Eudia Overview

Open ACV, total revenue, CLO engagement, marquee/velocity flags.

New Opportunity button sits at the top of every Account page. Click it to start a new deal for this company. You can also use the "New Opportunity" tab in the nav bar.

Contact Roles Matter

Add contacts from the Account to your Opportunities as Contact Roles (Champion, Economic Buyer, Decision Maker). This tracks who's involved in each deal and feeds into MEDDICC qualification.

Step 2
Creating an Opportunity
When you click "New Opportunity" from the Account page or the nav bar, you'll see this form. Fill in the required fields and hit Create.

Account *

Auto-filled if you came from an Account page. Otherwise, search and select.

Stage *

Start at S1 Discovery. Don't skip stages -- each has specific qualification criteria.

Product Lines *

Check all products in scope. Multi-select. Each product creates a separate Delivery at S4.

ACV + Source

Annual value in USD. Opportunity Source is required -- Inbound, Outbound, Events, Referral, or Existing Customer.

Step 3
Working the Deal
Your deal workspace. Stage bar at the top, key fields on the right, Contact Roles on the left, Product Lines below.

Stage Bar

Visual S1 → S6. Click "Mark Stage as Complete" to advance. Current stage is highlighted blue.

Contact Roles

Matt Gilmartin (Champion, SVP Deputy GC), Lynn Lugo (VP Assoc GC), Melissa Woodard (Dir Legal Ops). Always add roles -- this drives MEDDICC.

Key Fields

ACV ($120K), Target Sign (5/29), FC (Pipeline), Term (36 mo), Next Steps, Days in Stage (91). TCV auto-calculates.

Product Lines

AI Platform - Insights + Sigma selected. Each creates a separate Delivery record at S4 and a Product line item below.

Stage Milestones: S2 (SQO) = Qualified opportunity with identified pain. S4 (Proposal) = CS Staffing gate -- Customer Success reviews the deal and Deliveries auto-create (one per product). S5 (Negotiation) = Terms under review. S6 (Won) = Contract + Deal Code auto-generated.

Step 4
MEDDICC + Next Steps
Track deal qualification rigorously. Every MEDDICC field, customer history entry, and blocker feeds into Pipeline Review and GTM Brain intelligence.

Metrics (M)

Quantified pain: "contracting process is fragmented and inefficient, lacking centralized storage, visibility, and tools."

Decision Criteria (D)

What they need: centralized repository, metadata extraction, AI deduplication, contract analytics, version control.

Identify Pain (I)

Specific problems: no central contract location, dispersed data, rising QC costs, auditing difficulties. Direct from the customer.

Champion (C)

"Matt Gilmartin (SVP, Deputy General Counsel)" -- identified with title and role in the deal.

Customer History + Next Steps: Your running deal log. Add dated entries each time you engage. Format: "2026-02-06: 2/1 JH: Pushed opp into May as not getting feedback on RFP status." This syncs to Pipeline Review and is queryable by GTM Brain.

Step 5
Delivery (Auto-Created at S4)
When a deal reaches Stage 4 (Proposal), Deliveries auto-create -- one per product line. Multiple products = multiple Deliveries, each tracked separately.

One Delivery per Product

AppliedAI has 4 products, so 4 Deliveries were created: DEL-0231 (Sigma), DEL-0232 (Insights), DEL-0233 (Contracting), DEL-0234 (Compliance).

Delivery Details

Product Line, Delivery Model (AI-First 95%/5% Human), Phase (Month 1 - Setup), Status (Planning), Kickoff Date, Account link, Opportunity link.

Step 6
Contract (Closed Won)
When a deal closes, a Contract is created with line items, total value, term, and start/end dates. Links back to the Opportunity and Deliveries.

Contract Line Items

One per product: AI Contracting - Technology, AI Compliance - Technology, AI M&A - Managed Services. Each tracks quantity and value.

Term + Value

Contract Term (36 months), Total Contract Value ($600K), Start/End dates, Currency (USD), Contract Type (Recurring).

BOI Deal Code

Every Closed Won deal gets a unique BOI code (BOI-001, BOI-002...). Renewals inherit the parent code + suffix (BOI-001-01). Finance uses this for Campfire revenue mapping.

Renewal Tracking

The Renewal Opp field links renewal opportunities back to the original deal. BOI codes chain together for full revenue lineage.

Daily View
Pipeline Review
All active deals across the team with week-over-week change tracking. Filter by pod, stage, product, or target date. Click any cell to edit directly.

Inline editing: Click ACV, Target Date, Stage, FC, Products, or Next Steps to edit directly in the report. Changes save to Salesforce immediately. Collapse/expand rep sections by clicking the header bar.

Q1 Targeting
Accounts & Targets
Your Q1 account book. See assignments, outreach tiers (T1 Exec vs T2), geographic spread, and Eudia positioning context. Drop accounts to the pool or claim new ones.

Account Distribution

Every BL's Q1 book size by US/EU Pod. Front Book (pipeline + existing) vs Back Book (targeting). T1 Exec (leadership outreach) vs T2 (BL-driven).

Expanded View

Click your name to see every account with HQ, Industry, Revenue, Eudia Positioning Context, Status, Book, and Tier. Drop or Transfer from here.

Target Pool

700+ accounts available for claiming. Filter by Tier, Pod, Industry, State. Claim to add to your book (pod-restricted).

Weekly Focus

Recommended weekly targeting per rep. Accounts clustered by geography or industry. Swap accounts in/out of your weekly focus.
