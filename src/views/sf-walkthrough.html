Accounts

Every company we engage with has an Account record. Here's what the key fields mean.

Key Account Fields

FieldWhat It Means
Customer TypeNew, Existing, or Prospect. Determines how we approach the account.
IndustryCompany's primary industry (e.g., Financial Services, Healthcare & Pharma).
OwnerThe Business Lead responsible for this account.
Company RevenueExternal revenue data (e.g., $67.7B). Used for prioritization and tier assignment.
Outreach Tier NEWT1 Exec = Top 10-15 accounts where leadership initiates outreach. T2 = BL-driven prospecting.
Q1 Target BookChecked if this account is in your active Q1 focus list.
In Target PoolChecked if this account is available for claiming from the Target Pool.
Target PodUS Pod or EU Pod assignment.
Eudia Positioning ContextAI-researched brief on why this company is relevant — legal/compliance activity, M&A, regulatory exposure.
Finding Your Accounts

Navigate to Accounts & Targets in the top nav bar. Click your name in the Account Distribution tab to see your full Q1 book with tier assignments, geographic spread, and industry breakdown.

Opportunities

Opportunities track active deals from prospecting through close. Every deal gets a stage, ACV, and target sign date.

Key Opportunity Fields

FieldWhat It Means
ACVAnnual Contract Value — the dollar amount of the deal.
StageCurrent deal stage (S0 through S6). See progression below.
Target LOI DateWhen we expect the deal to close (Letter of Intent / signature target).
Product LineWhich Eudia products are in scope: AI Contracting, AI Compliance, Sigma, Insights, Litigation, M&A, FDE, Secondee.
Forecast CategoryPipeline = early/working. Gut = BL has confidence. Commit = expected to close this quarter.
Next StepsThe immediate next action for this deal.
Opportunity SourceHow the deal originated: Inbound, Outbound, Events, Referral, Existing Customer. Required field.
BOI Deal Code NEWAuto-generated unique ID on close (e.g., BOI-417). Used by Finance/Campfire for revenue mapping. Renewals inherit parent code (BOI-417-01).
Renewal OppLinks a renewal opportunity to its parent deal. Campfire uses this for renewal chain tracking.

Stage Progression

Deals move left to right. Each stage has specific criteria for advancement.

S0 Prospecting → S1 Discovery → S2 SQO → S3 Pilot → S4 Proposal → S5 Negotiation → S6 Closed Won
BOI Code Auto-Generated on Close

When a deal moves to Stage 6 Closed Won, a unique BOI Deal Code is automatically assigned (BOI-001, BOI-002, etc.). No manual input needed. Renewals get the parent code + suffix (BOI-001-01).

Creating a New Opportunity

1

Navigate to the Account

Go to the Account record page for the company.

2

Click "New Opportunity"

In the top nav bar, or from the Opportunities related list on the Account page.

3

Fill Required Fields

Name (format: "Company - Product"), Stage, Close Date, ACV, Opportunity Source (Inbound/Outbound/Events/Referral/Existing Customer).

4

Set Product Line

Select the product(s) in scope. Multi-select if multiple products.

5

Save

The opp appears in your Pipeline Review immediately.

Pipeline Review

The Pipeline Review tab shows all active deals across the team with week-over-week change tracking. Updated live from Salesforce.

What You See

  • Highlights bar — Stage advances, ACV changes, target slips, new deals this week
  • Stats bar — Total deals, pipeline value, weighted ACV, commit totals, AI-enabled metrics
  • Owner sections — Each rep's deals grouped together. Click the header to collapse/expand.

Columns

ColumnDetails
AccountCompany name + AI badge + customer type badge. Links to the opportunity record.
ACVAnnual Contract Value. Click to edit.
Δ ACVWeek-over-week ACV change. Green = increased. Red = decreased.
TargetTarget sign date. Click to edit.
Δ DaysTarget date slip/pull. Red = slipped. Green = pulled in.
StageCurrent stage (S0-S5). Arrow badge shows if advanced/regressed this week. Click to edit.
ProductsProduct line(s). Shows "2 products" for multi-select with hover tooltip. Click to edit.
FCForecast Category: Pipeline / Gut / Commit. Click to edit.
Next StepsCurrent next action for the deal. Truncated in view, full text on hover. Click to edit.
WoWWeek-over-week indicator: NEW, S2↑ (SQO'd), ★ (moved to Commit), ⚠ (risk), ✓ (positive change).

Filters

  • Pod — All, US, EU
  • Stage — All Stages, S1+, S2+, S3+, S4+
  • Products — Filter by specific product line
  • Target Sign — All Dates, This Quarter, Next Quarter, This FY
  • Changes Only — Show only deals with WoW changes
Inline Editing

Click any ACV, Target Date, Stage, FC, Products, or Next Steps cell to edit it directly. A small modal opens — make your change and hit Save. The value updates in Salesforce immediately.

Accounts & Targets

The Accounts & Targets tab has three views for managing your Q1 account book.

Tab 1: Q1 FY26 Account Distribution

Shows every BL's Q1 book size, organized by US Pod and EU Pod.

  • Q1 Book — Total accounts assigned for this quarter
  • Front Book — Accounts with active pipeline or existing customer status
  • Back Book — Targeting / cold outreach accounts
  • T1 Exec — Top 10-15 accounts where leadership initiates outreach
  • T2 BL — BL-driven prospecting accounts

Click your name to expand and see your full account list with:

  • HQ state, Industry, Revenue, Eudia Positioning Context
  • Status (Active Pipeline, Existing Customer, Prior Engagement, Cold)
  • T1/T2 toggle (click to reclassify, T1 capped at 15)
  • Drop button — Release an account to the Target Pool
  • Transfer button — Reassign to another BL
Dropping Accounts

When you drop an account with a negative reason (nurture, not a fit, no legal dept, etc.), it's excluded from the Target Pool entirely — not available for reassignment. Neutral drops (capacity, swapping) go to the pool.

Tab 2: Target Pool

Next-tranche accounts available for claiming when you work through your initial Q1 book.

  • Filters — Tier (T1/T2/T3), Pod, Industry (17 clean groupings), State, Search
  • Pool Tiers — Revenue-based: T1 = $10B+, T2 = $3-10B, T3 = under $3B
  • Claim — Add to your book (pod-restricted: US reps claim US Pod, EU reps claim EU Pod)
  • Assign — Admins can transfer to any BL

Tab 3: Weekly Focus

Recommended weekly targeting focus per rep. Accounts are clustered by geography or industry on alternating weeks.

  • All BLs preloaded — click to expand and see focus accounts
  • Swap — Replace a focus account with one from your back book
  • Shows the week date (e.g., "Week of February 17, 2026")

Key Workflows

Quick reference for the most common actions.

Creating a New Opportunity

Account page → New Opportunity → Fill Name, Stage, Close Date, ACV, Opportunity Source, Product Line → Save.

Moving a Deal Through Stages

Pipeline Review → Click the Stage cell → Select new stage → Save. Or edit directly on the Opportunity record page.

Dropping an Account

Accounts & Targets → Click your name → Find the account → Click Drop → Enter reason → Confirm. Negative reasons (nurture, not a fit) exclude from pool. Neutral reasons send to pool for others.

Claiming from Target Pool

Accounts & Targets → Target Pool tab → Filter by Tier/Industry/State → Click Claim. Auto-assigns T1 or T2 tier based on your current count. Pod-restricted (US reps can only claim US Pod accounts).

Recording a Meeting

Open Obsidian → Navigate to the account folder → Click the mic icon → Record your call → Stop when done → Review AI summary → Click "Sync to Salesforce" to push to the account record.
