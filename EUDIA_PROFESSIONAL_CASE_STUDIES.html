  • Contract Intelligence Case →
    Use for: Transportation, logistics, professional services, any high-volume contracting
    Trigger: Mentions contract backlog, procurement delays, OC costs for routine work
  • Revenue Enablement Case →

    Trigger: Legal as sales bottleneck, deals slipping across quarters, customer experience concerns
  • M&A Due Diligence Case →
    Use for: PE firms, active corporate acquirers, companies doing 2+ deals/year
    Trigger: Mentions M&A costs, deal velocity, competitive bid situations
  • Labor Arbitration Case →
    Use for: Companies with large workforces, union environments, labor-intensive industries
    Trigger: Mentions arbitration volume, hearing prep time, litigation support costs
  • Vendor Contracts Case →
    Use for: Retail, hospitality, any company with complex procurement
    Trigger: Internal complaints about legal turnaround, vendor onboarding delays
When to Share

Discovery Stage: Ask about their challenges first, then share case study in follow-up email that specifically addresses what they mentioned.

Qualification Stage: Use case studies to build credibility and help them visualize outcomes at their organization.

Proposal Stage: Attach relevant case study to proposal as proof of similar work. Use metrics to model their expected ROI.

Objection Handling: "Too expensive" → Show payback period from case study. "Unproven" → Share customer quotes and outcomes.

Customization Tips
  • Replace "Fortune 500/Fortune 50/Fortune 10" with actual company name ONLY if you have written approval to reference publicly
  • Adjust metrics slightly if you have more recent/accurate data from customer
  • Add industry-specific details relevant to your prospect (e.g., mention "GDPR compliance" for European companies)
  • Include customer reference offer: "Happy to connect you with [Title] who can speak to [specific outcome]"

All case studies follow Eudia's 2025 positioning:

  • Augmented Intelligence: Emphasize humans + AI, not AI replacing humans
  • Company Brain: Highlight institutional knowledge capture and compounding value
