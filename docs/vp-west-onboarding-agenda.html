VP West Coast Onboarding Call — Agenda

Duration: 30-45 min

Prep: He's reviewed the Sales Process doc

Goal: Orient him to how we operate, set expectations, build working relationship

1. Intros (5 min)

  • Your background, role as Chief of Staff / GTM & Revenue
  • His background, what drew him to Eudia
  • How you'll work together (you = RevOps partner, not blocker)

2. Sales Process — Quick Walkthrough (10-12 min)

Anchor on the doc he's already reviewed. Don't re-read it — hit the "why" behind key points.

Topic What to Cover Time
Stage Discipline Why accurate staging matters (forecast, investor reporting). Stage 2 = SQO, real pipeline. 2 min
MEDDPICC Our qualification framework. Not optional — tied to forecast confidence. 2 min
Key Fields Revenue Type, Sales Type, Product Line — these drive reporting. Garbage in = garbage out. 3 min
New vs. Expansion vs. Renewal How we classify deals. Impacts how we report growth. 2 min
Delivery Handoff Post-close, what happens. (Future state: PM platform sync.) 2 min
Pause for questions.

3. Tools & Systems (5 min)

Tool What It Does His Interaction
Salesforce Source of truth for pipeline, accounts, opps Daily
GTM Brain (Slack) Quick lookups, weekly snapshot, pipeline intel As needed
Hypernote Meeting notes → auto-summarized, synced to SF Post-meetings
Campfire ERP for contracts/ARR (Finance-owned) Awareness only

He doesn't need to be a power user on day one. Just knows what exists.

4. Weekly Rhythm & Reporting (5 min)

  • Weekly Snapshot — PDF that goes out with pipeline health, closed deals, forecast
  • BL Pipeline Report — His team's deals, by stage, by rep
  • Forecast Check-ins — Cadence TBD, but expect stage + close date accuracy
Key message: We're building a data-driven culture. Accuracy = trust = autonomy.

5. How I Can Help (3 min)

  • RevOps support: reports, data cleanup, process questions
  • Escalation path for deal blockers or cross-functional issues
  • Open door for feedback on what's working / what's broken

6. Open Discussion (5-10 min)

Prompt questions:

  • "What's unclear or feels off based on what you've seen so far?"
  • "What worked well at your last company that we should consider?"
  • "Any early observations on the team or pipeline?"

7. Closing (2 min)

  • Confirm follow-up items (if any)
  • Offer to connect again in 1-2 weeks after he's ramped
  • Remind him: Slack you anytime
Tone reminder: He's experienced — treat him like a peer, not a trainee. You're orienting, not lecturing.
